Prevention

Enter your age range:













During your 20's

Being a teenager doesn’t mean that you’re free froma heart disease. Staying strictly by the proverb”prevention is better than cure” it is advisable to keep a heart disease far away from you at this tender age. Choose a healthy eating plan choose foods that are light and fiberous. Eventhough,finger-smacking it is advisable to keep junkfoods andother fatty foods away. Eat lot of vegetables and fruits. Be physically active.  Do some bit of dance moves to your favourite tune. Or get out and breathe in some fresh air by some brisk walking regularly. Learn the symptoms of heart attack and stroke

During your 30's

Make heart-healthy living a family affair. Tarin your kids and other family members to have a heart healthy lifestyle. Know your family history. Having a relative with heart disease increases your risk, and more so if the relative is a parent or sibling. Tame your stress Long-term stress causes an increase in heart rate and blood pressure that may damage the artery walls.

During your 40's

Watch your weight. You may notice your metabolism slowing down at this age. Manage weight by healthy diet and exercise. Have your blood sugar level checked. Keep a check on snoring

During your 50's

Eat a healthy diet.  Learn the warning signs of hear attack. Follow your treatment plan. Lower your risk by following your prescribed treatment plan, including medications and lifestyle and diet changes.

During your 60's and above

Have an ankle-brachial index text The test assesses the pulses in the feet to help diagnose peripheral artery disease(PAD), a lesser-known cardiovascular disease in which plaque builds up in the leg arteries. Watch your weight. Learn the warning signs of heart attack and stroke.
